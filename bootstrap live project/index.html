About F1

Formula 1 History


Formula 1, often abbreviated as F1, is the highest class of international single-seater auto racing sanctioned by the FIA (Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile). It originated in the early 20th century but was officially established in 1950 with the inaugural World Championship of Drivers. The first race took place at Silverstone, UK, and was won by Italian driver Giuseppe Farina in an Alfa Romeo. The 1950s were dominated by legendary teams like Ferrari, Alfa Romeo, and Maserati, and featured iconic drivers such as Juan Manuel Fangio, who won five world titles during that era. The 1960s saw technical innovations, including rear-engine designs, and drivers like Jim Clark and Graham Hill rose to prominence. Safety was not yet a priority, leading to frequent fatal accidents. In the 1970s, driver safety began to improve with input from stars like Jackie Stewart, who also won three championships. The rivalry between Niki Lauda and James Hunt brought drama and media attention to the sport.
